Latin and Greek Number Roots

English is composed of many languages. In this lesson, I’m covering the number systems of Latin and Greek – two languages from which we derive many words, especially in science. In addition to memorizing the prefixes and looking at the word examples, I encourage you to think about how each of these words relates to the number.

Example:
semi – half; semicircle – half a circle.
uni- one; unicycle – one wheel (compare with bicycle and tricycle).

Caution: not all words that appear to have these prefixes are related to the word. For example – “center” looks like it has the “cent” (100) root from Latin, but is actually derived from a Greek word meaning “sharp point”.

Note: there are many more numbers but I have only included the ones I feel are in common use. There are far more that you will come across if you are in a scientific field. Why do they use Latin and Greek in science so much? They are dead languages. Once you realize the interconnectedness and roots of languages you may find that you are in fact polyglot – congratulations!
