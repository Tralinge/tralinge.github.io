English Corner

Why Learn English?

So you’ve decided to learn English, one of the most prolific languages in the world, with official status in 67 countries, around 400 million native speakers, and roughly 1.5 billion speakers world-wide. It’s the language of finance and a great advantage, whether you want to read a scientific paper or travel to a foreign country, English will likely be very useful in your life. Unfortunately, it can be difficult to obtain fluency in English. This page is dedicated to exploring the difficulties of learning English, tips to help you improve, worksheets to help you practice, and a section where you can ask your own questions.

Pronunciation

Etymology – Study of the Origin of Words
As noted in the History of the English Language, English is derived from several languages. If you spend a little time learning some of the roots of these other languages, many of the seemingly complicated, high-level words in English will suddenly start to seem very reasonable and easy to remember, now that you have made the connection. With a little bit of practice, you will be surprised how many new words you will understand without even using a dictionary. Finally, once you realize the interrelatedness of languages you will find you are well on your way to becoming a polyglot! Learn some word roots here.
